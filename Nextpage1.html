• YOUNG ADULT AND MAJOR TURNING POINTS

At 18, we moved again — this time to Port Harcourt, Rivers State. I did a diploma there and worked small freelance gigs. In 2024, at 19, I got admitted into the Federal University of Technology Owerri, FUTO, for MSc Computer Science. Yeah, I jumped straight in after a fast-tracked BSc. It felt like coming home to Imo, but with a bigger goal. The turning point was year 1 of my MSc, when I joined a research group working on AI for agricultural yield prediction. My thesis is on that now. It’s slower than I expected. Some weeks I question if I’m cut out for research. Other weeks, a model works and I forget to sleep.

  • ADULTHOOD/CAREER AND PERSONAL GROWTH

Right now, I’m finishing my MSc coursework and working on my thesis. I haven’t started a full-time career yet, but I freelance on web and mobile projects to keep the lights on. What drives me is simple: I love God, and I want my work to reflect that. I’m super hardworking, sometimes to a fault — I’ll debug code till 3 AM. About the girls thing: it hasn’t stopped. Even in grad school, people assume I’m flirting because I’m friendly. I’ve learned to set boundaries early and laugh it off. Faith and focus are non-negotiable for me. Biggest struggle: staying patient when progress feels slow, and not letting people’s assumptions frustrate me. I’ve learned talent without discipline burns out fast.

  • REFLECTIONS AND THEMES

Three things keep showing up: faith, discipline, and movement. I’ve lived in three states, and every move taught me to adapt and trust God more. If I could tell 18-year-old Augustine one thing, it’d be: stop trying to prove yourself to people. Protect your focus, even if they don’t believe you. The pattern is this — I grow most when I’m uncomfortable, whether that’s in a new city or stuck on a hard algorithm.
