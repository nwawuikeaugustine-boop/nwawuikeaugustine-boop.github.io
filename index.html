THE AUTOBIOGRAPHY OF GREAT VENERABLE
NWAWUIKE AUGUSTINE

      INTRODUCTION


I’m writing this at 21, sitting in Owerri with my laptop open and Proverbs 3:5 stuck on my wall. I’m still in school, still learning, and still figuring out how to say no to distractions. This is the story of my first 21 years — born in the north, raised in the east, studying in the south. If you want a perfect story, this isn’t it. If you want to see how faith, discipline, and a jovial attitude can get you through moves, doubts, and a lot of unwanted DMs, stay.

  • ORIGINS

I was born on May 19, 2005, in Kaduna, northern Nigeria. My dad worked in logistics, my mum was a nurse. When I was 7, we relocated to Ngor-Okpala LGA in Imo State, where my family is from. That’s where I really grew up — red soil, compound houses, and Sundays that started at 6 AM for church. I’m the youngest of four. My older siblings still treat me like the baby, even though I’m 6 feet tall now. They tease me about how I’d “teach” them coding using sticks and sand as a kid. Earliest memory: sitting on the veranda in Kaduna, watching my dad fix a car alternator while he hummed gospel songs. That mix of work and worship stuck with me.

  • ADOLESCENCE/EDUCATION

Secondary school was in Owerri, Imo State. I got serious about grades, but more than that, I got serious about God. Youth fellowship on Fridays kept me grounded. Here’s the funny part: even in SS2, girls in school and from other schools would throw advances my way. I’d dodge them, tell them I was focused on God and school. Nobody believed me because I’m jovial and easy to talk to. They figured “he’s just playing hard to get.” I wasn’t. At 16, I failed Further Maths and thought it was over. My pastor said, “Augustine, God didn’t call you to be perfect. He called you to be faithful.” That stuck. Outside class, I was always tinkering — first with phones, then with HTML and Python tutorials I downloaded at night when data was cheap.
